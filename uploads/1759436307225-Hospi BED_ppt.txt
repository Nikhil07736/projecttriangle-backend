### **Slide 2: The Problem**

## The High Cost of Operational Blind Spots

Manual bed management creates critical inefficiencies that impact your entire hospital.

*   **Inefficient Patient Flow & ER Boarding**
    *   Lengthy delays in moving patients from the ER to inpatient rooms due to uncertainty about bed availability.

*   **Critical Communication Gaps**
    *   Reliance on phone calls, whiteboards, and manual data entry leads to errors and wasted time for nursing, transport, and housekeeping staff.

*   **Significant Revenue Loss**
    *   An empty bed that isn't designated as "clean and available" is a lost opportunity. Delays in bed turnover directly translate to lower patient throughput and lost revenue.

*   **Staff Burnout & Patient Dissatisfaction**
    *   Nurses spend valuable time tracking down beds instead of on patient care, leading to frustration and impacting HCAHPS scores.

---
**--- Speaker Notes ---**

"The core problem isn't a lack of beds; it's a lack of real-time information. When a patient is discharged, a complex chain of events is supposed to happen, but it's often broken by communication gaps.

Your nurses are on the phone trying to confirm if a room is clean. Your housekeeping team is working off an outdated list. And all the while, patients are waiting in the ER for hours, which we know negatively impacts clinical outcomes and patient satisfaction.

This isn't just an operational headache; it's a financial drain. Industry data suggests that the average hospital loses millions annually due to inefficient patient flow. We're here to fix that."
